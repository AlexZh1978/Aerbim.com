The "Visualization Settings" command

Purpose: enable the user to customize the visualization of marking: the location relative to the insertion point, the distance from the mark to the insertion point, the size of the family for visualization, the visualization step.


Fig. 1. Launching the "Visualization Settings" command

To run the command, click the "Visualization Settings" button, which is located in the hidden part on the "Marking" panel of the "3D Security Designer" tab. After that, the "Mark Visualization Settings" window will be displayed.


Fig. 2. The "Mark Visualization Settings" window

The window controls elements are responsible for the following functionality:

  1. The drop-down list "Choose Type (type size)" allows you to select one of the four predefined sizes of the annotation family, according to which the marking will be visualized.


Fig. 3. Types (type sizes) of annotations

Fig. 4. Appearance of annotation types (type sizes)

  1. The "Type description (of a type size)" field gives a description of the type size selected in the drop-down list (1).
  2. The "Location" drop-down list allows you to select the location of the marking visualization relative to the insertion point of the element (family) of the marked circuit.


Fig. 5. The "Location" list

The following options are available for positioning the marking visualization relative to the insertion point of the element:

  • "Top" — the visualization of the mark is located at the top relative to the insertion point of the element (family).


Fig. 6. Visualization of the mark located at the top relative to the insertion point of the element (family)

  • "Bottom" — the visualization of the mark is located at the bottom relative to the insertion point of the element (family).


Fig. 7. Visualization of the mark located at the bottom relative to the insertion point of the element (family)

  • "Left" — the visualization of the mark is located on the left relative to the insertion point of the element (family).


Fig. 8. Visualization of the mark located on the left relative to the insertion point of the element (family)

  • "Right" — the visualization of the mark is located on the right relative to the insertion point of the element (family).


Fig. 9. Visualization of the mark located on the right relative to the insertion point of the element (family)

  • "Top left" — visualization of the mark is located on the top left relative to the insertion point of the element (family).


Fig. 10. Visualization of the mark located on the top left relative to the insertion point of the element (family)

  • "Top right" — visualization of the mark is located on the top right relative to the insertion point of the element (family).


Fig. 11. Visualization of the mark located on the top right relative to the insertion point of the element (family)

  • "Bottom left" — visualization of the mark is located on the bottom left relative to the insertion point of the element (family).


Fig. 12. Visualization of the mark located on the bottom left relative to the insertion point of the element (family)

  • "Bottom right" — visualization of the mark is located on the bottom right relative to the insertion point of the element (family).


Fig. 13. Visualization of the mark located on the bottom right relative to the insertion point of the element (family)

  1. If the "With a Leader" checkbox is selected, the visualization of the mark will be displayed with a visualization line from element.


Fig. 14. Visualization of mark with a line from element

  1. The "Radius" field allows you to set the distance (by radius) from the beginning of the visualization line to the insertion point of the element (family) of the marked circuit. The radius is limited to three digits and cannot be less than one. The radius is measured in the set project units for the length.
  2. The "Visualization Step" field allows you to set the step according to which the marking of elements (families) in the marked circuit will be visualized. An example of marking visualization for a visualization step equal to two (visualization is displayed for every second element (family) of the circuit, starting from the first.


Fig. 15. Example of visualization of marking for a visualization step equal to two (visualization is displayed for every second element (family) of the circuit, starting from the first)

  1. The "OK" button sets the visualization settings, according to which the marking of the elements (families) in the marked circuits will be displayed in the future and closes the "Mark Visualization Settings" window.
  2. The "Cancel" button cancels the "Visualization Settings" command and closes the "Mark Visualization Settings" window.

If the command fails, a corresponding message will be displayed, while the marking visualization settings will not be saved.


Fig. 16. Failure message
